Projects

The UC OSPO Network Survey: perspectives from open source contributors at the University of California

As the Open Source Programs Specialist at the University of California, Santa Barbara, I am leading a multi-campus survey of more than 200 open source contributors affiliated with the University of California (UC). The resulting publication will help Open Source Program Offices around the world determine how to allocate their services, and it will also help new open source support programs take root at UC campuses.

Check out the UC OSPO Network website.

Distributing discovery: a qualitative survey of Janelia's data culture, and a plan for action

As a Scientific Computing Associate at the Howard Hughes Medical Institute's Janelia Research Campus, I interviewed 60 scientists about their data management habits and needs. I presented my findings in an internal report and a presentation to an audience of several hundred at an internal symposium. This work helped initiate the reorganization of the Library into the Data and Information Services department.
